About Me

I am an undergraduate student at the Future Technology School, Shenzhen Technology University, expecting my Bachelor of Engineering degree in July 2026. I am also an X Scholar at the Tsinghua University Tsien Excellence in Engineering Program - Shenzhen X-Institute.

My research is centered on the design and application of novel robotic mechanisms and advanced spectroscopy techniques. My recent work includes developing underactuated robotic grippers based on the Peaucellier linkage, which has resulted in three accepted papers at premier IEEE conferences (ARM, CASE, and IROS 2025). I also have hands-on experience in utilizing excimer lasers for Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy (LIBS) and developing automated detection workflows with LabVIEW.

Research Interests: Robotics and Mechatronics, Underactuated Mechanisms, Gripper Design, Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy (LIBS), and Automated Systems.
